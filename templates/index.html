Radiation emitted by a source at given surface temperature

The black body model is widely applied in many different contexts: from the spectral shape of the radiation emitted by the Sun—which closely resembles a black body spectrum at a temperature of around 6000 K—to LED lights used for illumination, which are available in both warm (low source temperature) and cold (high source temperature) color tones.

\( B(\lambda, T) = \frac{2hc}{\lambda^3} \frac{1}{e^{\frac{hc}{k_B T\lambda}} - 1} \)
